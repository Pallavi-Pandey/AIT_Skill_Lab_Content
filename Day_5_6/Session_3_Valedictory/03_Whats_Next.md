# What's Next

> **Your bootcamp ends today. Your analytics career starts tomorrow.** This guide gives you a concrete map of what to learn, where to learn it, and how to get hired as a data analyst.

---

## The Honest Truth About Where You Stand

After five days, you are not an expert. You are not supposed to be. Here is what you genuinely are:

- You have a working mental model of the full analytics pipeline from raw data to business decision.
- You have built one real project end-to-end that you can talk about in an interview.
- You have enough foundation to learn the next layer of every skill you touched — faster than someone starting from zero.
- You are dangerous enough to be useful on a real team with guidance.

The next 90 days determine how quickly you move from "useful with guidance" to "independently productive."

---

## Recommended Learning Paths

Choose the path that matches your career goal. You do not have to follow only one — but focus on one primary path first before branching.

### Path 1: Data Analyst (Industry Entry Role)
**Goal:** Land a Data Analyst role at a company within 6–12 months.

**Priority stack:**
1. SQL — Intermediate to Advanced (window functions, CTEs, subqueries)
2. Power BI or Tableau — Complete a real certification
3. Python / Pandas — Automation, data wrangling with larger datasets
4. Statistics — Confidence intervals, hypothesis testing, A/B testing basics
5. Excel / Google Sheets — Still heavily used; pivots, VLOOKUP, INDEX-MATCH

---

### Path 2: Business Intelligence Developer
**Goal:** Build enterprise dashboards and data pipelines as a specialist.

**Priority stack:**
1. Power BI with DAX — Advanced measures, calculated columns, row-level security
2. Data Warehousing concepts (Star Schema, Slowly Changing Dimensions)
3. SQL — Complex joins, stored procedures, performance tuning
4. Azure or AWS fundamentals (cloud data services are everywhere now)
5. Python for ETL automation (scheduling data pulls and transformations)

---

### Path 3: Data Scientist (Longer Journey — 12–24 months)
**Goal:** Build predictive models and statistical analysis capabilities.

**Priority stack:**
1. Statistics — Probability, distributions, regression, hypothesis testing
2. Python — Scikit-learn, NumPy advanced, feature engineering
3. Machine Learning — Supervised (regression, classification) then unsupervised (clustering)
4. SQL — Advanced querying and data extraction at scale
5. Storytelling — Data science without communication is useless

## Industry Certifications Worth Pursuing

These certifications are recognized by hiring managers and add credibility to your resume.

| Certification | Provider | Difficulty | Cost | Time to Prepare | Value |
|--------------|----------|-----------|------|----------------|-------|
| **Google Data Analytics Certificate** | Google / Coursera | Beginner | ~Rs. 3,000/month (Coursera) | 3–6 months | High — widely recognized for entry-level roles |
| **Microsoft PL-300 (Power BI Data Analyst)** | Microsoft | Intermediate | ~Rs. 4,500 exam fee | 2–3 months | Very High — direct for BI roles |
| **Tableau Desktop Specialist** | Tableau / Salesforce | Intermediate | ~$250 USD | 2–3 months | High — Tableau-specific roles |
| **Microsoft DP-900 (Azure Data Fundamentals)** | Microsoft | Beginner | ~Rs. 3,800 exam fee | 1 month | Good foundation for cloud analytics |
| **AWS Cloud Practitioner** | Amazon | Beginner | ~$100 USD | 1–2 months | Good for cloud-adjacent analytics roles |
| **Kaggle Python / Pandas / SQL Certifications** | Kaggle | Beginner–Intermediate | Free | 1–4 weeks each | Great starting proof of competence |

**Recommendation for most students:** Start with **Kaggle free courses** (Python, SQL, Pandas — each takes 2–4 hours) to get verifiable certificates quickly, then invest in the **Google Data Analytics Certificate** for depth, then the **PL-300** once Power BI becomes your primary tool.

## Portfolio Building Tips

A portfolio is what gets you a call back when your resume gets you past the filter. For data analytics, your portfolio is a collection of projects that demonstrate your end-to-end capability.

**What to build:**

| Project Type | Why It Matters | Example |
|-------------|---------------|---------|
| **End-to-end data story** | Shows you can go from raw data to business insight | "Analyzing Zomato Restaurant Data to identify expansion opportunities in Tier-2 cities" |
| **Dashboard (public)** | Publishable on Tableau Public or Power BI Community | "IPL Performance Dashboard — 10 seasons, interactive player comparison" |
| **SQL portfolio** | Shows structured data proficiency | "HR Analytics SQL Queries — 25 queries across attendance, attrition, and payroll data" |
| **Python notebook** | Shows coding and EDA skills | "EDA of 5 lakh Amazon product reviews — sentiment patterns and rating distributions" |

**Where to host your portfolio:**
- **GitHub** — For Python scripts and notebooks (industry standard, everyone will check this)
- **Tableau Public** — For Tableau dashboards (free, public, shareable link)
- **Power BI Community** — For Power BI reports
- **Notion or a personal website** — To link all projects in one place

**Portfolio rule of thumb:** 3 strong, polished, well-documented projects beat 15 half-finished ones. Quality signals more than quantity.

## Job Search Tips for Data Analysts

### What to Include on Your Resume
- One bullet per project that follows: "Built [what] using [tools] to [achieve what outcome]."
- Example: "Built an interactive sales dashboard in Power BI using 3 years of transaction data to identify Rs. 12L monthly revenue leakage across 5 product categories."
- List certifications with the month/year of completion.
- Link to your GitHub and/or Tableau Public profile.

### Where to Apply
- **LinkedIn** — Set your profile to "Open to Work" in the data analytics/BI category.
- **Naukri.com / Internshala** — Actively used by Indian companies and startups for analyst roles.
- **AngelList / Wellfound** — Startups, often more open to freshers with portfolios than large companies.
- **Direct apply to company websites** — Look for "Business Analyst", "Data Analyst", "BI Developer" roles.

### What to Prepare for Interviews
- 2–3 SQL queries you can write on a whiteboard (GROUP BY, JOIN, subquery)
- An explanation of your capstone project in 2 minutes
- The answer to: "Walk me through how you would approach a messy dataset for the first time."
- Basic statistics: What is mean vs. median? When would you use one over the other?

## One Final Thought

Every professional data analyst you admire was once exactly where you are right now — they had a foundation, a capstone project, and a map. The only thing that separated them from where they started is showing up consistently over the next 90 days.

The tools will change. SQL, Power BI, Python — these will evolve. What will not change is the fundamental skill of taking a messy problem, finding clarity in data, and communicating it in a way that helps humans make better decisions. That is what you learned. That is what is yours to keep.

Go build something.
