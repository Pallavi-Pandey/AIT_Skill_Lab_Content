# Jury Evaluation Rubric

> **Purpose:** This rubric is used by the evaluation panel to score each team's capstone presentation. It is published in advance so every student knows exactly what they are being assessed on and can prepare accordingly.

---

## Overview of Evaluation Criteria

The capstone is scored out of **100 points** across five criteria. Each criterion is weighted to reflect the skills that matter most in a professional data analytics role.

| # | Criterion | Weight | Max Score |
|---|-----------|--------|-----------|
| 1 | Dashboard Quality | 30% | 30 |
| 2 | Insight Depth & Accuracy | 25% | 25 |
| 3 | Presentation Clarity & Communication | 20% | 20 |
| 4 | Teamwork & Role Distribution | 15% | 15 |
| 5 | Q&A Response & Critical Thinking | 10% | 10 |
| | **Total** | **100%** | **100** |

---

## Scoring Levels

Each criterion is scored on a 0–10 scale (before applying the weight multiplier):

| Score | Label | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | Excellent | Exceeds expectations; industry-ready standard |
| 7–8 | Good | Meets all core expectations with minor gaps |
| 5–6 | Satisfactory | Meets basic expectations but lacks depth or polish |
| 3–4 | Needs Improvement | Below expectations; significant gaps present |
| 0–2 | Insufficient | Does not meet the minimum requirement |

## Detailed Scoring Rubric

### Criterion 1: Dashboard Quality (Weight: 30%)

> Assesses the technical completeness, accuracy, and visual professionalism of the BI dashboard.

| Score Band | Descriptor |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Excellent (27–30)** | Dashboard has KPI cards, trend charts, and breakdown visuals. All slicers are functional and cross-filter correctly. Consistent color theme, descriptive titles, formatted data labels. File loads without errors. Numbers match raw data when cross-checked. Team name and project title visible. |
| **Good (21–26)** | Most required elements present. 1–2 minor visual inconsistencies (e.g., font size varies, one slicer slow to respond). All numbers are accurate. |
| **Satisfactory (15–20)** | Dashboard has charts and filters but lacks polish. Missing KPI cards or trend view. Some data labels absent. Numbers are largely accurate but one metric could not be validated by jury. |
| **Needs Improvement (9–14)** | Dashboard is functional but incomplete. Missing multiple required elements. Visual design is inconsistent. At least one metric appears inaccurate. |
| **Insufficient (0–8)** | Dashboard is largely incomplete, non-functional, or contains significant data errors. Not suitable for a business audience. |

### Criterion 2: Insight Depth & Accuracy (Weight: 25%)

> Assesses the quality of the analytical findings: are they correct, meaningful, and actionable?

| Score Band | Descriptor |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Excellent (23–25)** | 3 or more validated, non-obvious insights. Each follows the What/So What/Now What structure. Insights reflect critical thinking (e.g., identifies root cause, not just symptom). Business value is quantified (Rs., %, time saved). Limitations acknowledged. |
| **Good (18–22)** | 2–3 valid insights with clear business relevance. "So What?" is articulated for each. Findings are correct but one could be deeper or more specific. |
| **Satisfactory (13–17)** | 2 insights presented but they describe the chart rather than the business implication. "So What?" is weak or absent for at least one finding. |
| **Needs Improvement (8–12)** | Insights are superficial (e.g., "Sales were highest in December") with no business interpretation. No "Now What?" recommendations offered. |
| **Insufficient (0–7)** | Insights are absent, incorrect, or directly contradict the data shown on the dashboard. |

### Criterion 3: Presentation Clarity & Communication (Weight: 20%)

> Assesses the structure, clarity, and delivery of the oral presentation.

| Score Band | Descriptor |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Excellent (18–20)** | Presentation follows Minto Pyramid structure (Hook → Evidence → Solution → Value). Opening creates immediate engagement. Story is cohesive — each slide leads logically to the next. Speaks to the audience, not the screen. Finishes within the 10-minute window. Slides are clean and minimal. |
| **Good (14–17)** | Clear structure with a defined opening, body, and close. Good eye contact and pacing. 1–2 slides are text-heavy or the opening is generic ("Good morning, we are Group X"). Finishes within time. |
| **Satisfactory (10–13)** | Structure is present but not Minto-based. Team reads from slides. Story has gaps between sections. Time management is slightly off (over or under by 2+ minutes). |
| **Needs Improvement (5–9)** | No clear structure. Multiple members read directly from the screen. Story is hard to follow. Significant time management issues. |
| **Insufficient (0–4)** | Presentation is disorganized, incoherent, or not delivered in the allocated time. |

### Criterion 4: Teamwork & Role Distribution (Weight: 15%)

> Assesses whether every member contributed meaningfully and whether the team functioned as a unit.

| Score Band | Descriptor |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Excellent (14–15)** | All members present distinct sections confidently. Transitions between speakers are smooth and rehearsed. Roles (Data Engineer, Visualizer, Analyst, PM) are evident from the presentation. Team handles unexpected moments (technical glitch, question) calmly and collectively. |
| **Good (11–13)** | All members speak. 1 member is slightly less confident than others. Transitions are mostly smooth. |
| **Satisfactory (8–10)** | All members speak but contribution is uneven. 1–2 members clearly dominate while others read brief sections. |
| **Needs Improvement (4–7)** | 1 member does not present, or the team has not clearly divided responsibilities. Team appears uncoordinated during the demo. |
| **Insufficient (0–3)** | Only 1–2 members present. Team is visibly unprepared for collaboration. |

### Criterion 5: Q&A Response & Critical Thinking (Weight: 10%)

> Assesses the team's ability to think on their feet, defend their analysis, and engage with challenges.

| Score Band | Descriptor |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Excellent (9–10)** | Answers are direct, specific, and reference the data. Team acknowledges limitations honestly without becoming defensive. "We don't know" is answered with a clear alternative or next step. Team invites deeper questions. |
| **Good (7–8)** | Most answers are clear and accurate. 1 question causes hesitation but is handled reasonably. Team is respectful and composed. |
| **Satisfactory (5–6)** | Some answers are vague or overly general. Team struggles with at least one analytical challenge question. |
| **Needs Improvement (2–4)** | Multiple questions are deflected or answered incorrectly. Team appears unprepared for scrutiny of their numbers. |
| **Insufficient (0–1)** | Team cannot answer basic questions about their own data or methodology. |
